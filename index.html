What's an NFT?

An NFT is a "Non-fungible Token"... a unique crypto token that can't be duplicated. It's basically a public certificate of ownership on a blockchain.


You may have heard of them on the news with some selling for tens of millions of dollars or of Twitter's founder making and selling an NFT of his first tweet.

What's BitSig?

BitSig enables new types of NFTs that can be signed for free. Digital objects are now signable, just like physical ones.


If someone important signs your NFT, it becomes more valuable just like a soccer ball does when your favorite soccer player signs it.


If you sign someone else's NFT, you can get a percentage of the NFT's sale, every time it's sold.

Community

Ultimately, what gives an NFT value is its community. BitSig makes the connection between a token and its community explicit. The more signatures a token has, the more valuable it is to the community.

How do I sign an NFT?

Signing an NFT with BitSig is very easy (and free), even your grandma could do it! Simply scan the QR code of the token with the app and it automatically signs it.


If you don't have the app, you can connect your wallet and sign it here.

If you're a fan...

If you're an artist/creator...

Connect with your fans by creating an NFT for them to sign. When you sell the NFT to a super fan, you can give back to the community of fans that supported you with a signers royalty.

If you're a conducting business...

Create an NFT of a contract and easily have it signed by all parties involved. The signatures will be on the NFT forever, even if BitSig.org shuts down.

How does it work?

When signing a token, the BitSig app (or your wallet) signs a message under the format:

"contractAddress=address&tokenId=ID&Timestamp=timestamp",

which is a unique identifier of the token. The message, digital signature, and your public key are added to a file holding all of the signatures so that anyone can verify them. https://etherscan.io/verifySig


The file that has all of the signatures is stored on ipfs. When more signers sign, a new file is created that includes the new signers. The new file has a link that points back to the previous file. BitSig creates the files and keeps track of the link to the latest file. The token itself can also hold the link to the latest file. By continuously setting the latest link in the token, a history of the signer files is held in the blockchain, which ensures the validity of the chain of files.


For extra security, signatures can be added directly to the token. This, however, will cost gas payable by either the token owner or the signer. This means that if bitsig.org ever goes down, your tokens will continue to be signable.
